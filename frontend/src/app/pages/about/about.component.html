About PawID

A community project for stray dog welfare

What is PawID?

PawID is a community-driven registry for stray dogs. It lets anyone register a stray dog they encounter, report follow-up sightings, and track the dog's movement and health over time. The goal is simple: give every stray dog a persistent identity so the community can watch out for them together.

Why it exists

Thousands of stray dogs roam city streets without any record of who they are, where they live, or whether they are healthy. Shelters and volunteers often encounter the same dog multiple times without knowing its history. PawID gives each dog a profile that grows with every community sighting — turning isolated encounters into a shared story.

How it works

When you spot a stray dog, search the registry first. If the dog is already registered, add a sighting — it takes under a minute. If the dog is new, create a profile with a photo, a rough description, and a location. Every report you submit is credited to your account and builds the dog's location trail on the map.

Who can contribute?

Anyone. Create a free account with just an email address and a display name. Your display name will appear publicly next to your sightings so other community members can see who is actively looking out for dogs in a given area. There are no moderators — the community self-organises around the data.

The technology

PawID is built with Angular on the frontend and Spring Boot on the backend. Maps are powered by Leaflet and OpenStreetMap. The project is intentionally kept simple and readable — it was designed as a learning platform as much as a real tool.
